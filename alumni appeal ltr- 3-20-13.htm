Dear Class Representatives and Fellow Alumni                                         March 20, 2013 

The ACS Alumni Association (ACSAA) is at an important crossroad.  Please take time to read this appeal for help to keep us going as an organization and pass on to your classmates and post in social media! 

The ACSAA has been struggling in calm seas for a while.   The heavy involvement of our most key volunteers in helping plan Reunion 2011 and the declining interest in keeping the ACSAA active has taken the wind out of our sails.  Over the years a few dedicated alumni have put in the most work to keep the ACSAA operating.  David Bishton ’72 founded the ACSAA in the late 1970’s as an informal organization and served as its President until the ACSAA was organized in 1999 with a Board of Directors operating under By Laws (can be read at www.acsaa.org)  . Key contributors since 1999 have been:  Bob Johnson ’64, ACSAA Board of Directors President from 1999 to 2008, current Secretary and Data Base, Website, Directory and Publications Manager; Joan (Lugo) Wilkinson-Steele ’62 Treasurer and board Member; Frances Milligan ’72, Gaucho Gazette Editor since the 1990’s and board member; and Michael Bishton ’70 who designed our Website, Data Base and Directory formats.    Our other current and past board members have provided valuable contributions over the years and dedicated alumni volunteers have chaired the 9 ACSAA reunions since 1982.     

The 9-member ACSAA Board of Directors had its four-year term expire earlier in 2012 but the By Laws permit existing members to voluntarily continue in place until such time that a new board can be elected.   Younger volunteer leaders (a minimum of 7) are the key in transitioning the ACSAA onto the Internet, and take advantage of social communications media such as Facebook and Twitter. 

Membership Status- The last set of publications was mailed out in early 2010 to then existing members.   All current members at that time and any alumni that have paid dues since then will have their remaining terms and dues credits kept intact. 

The ACSAA is pleased that Jade (York) Polk, longtime 1982 Class Rep has volunteered to fill the position of President and to help recruit alumni to serve on a new Board of Directors and take on the administrative duties needed to keep the ACSAA active. Claudia (Cansler) Zaunbrecher ‘68 has also agreed to chair the Nominating Committee and she needs four other volunteers to help recommend a slate of candidates.    If you are interested in being a candidate for the Board of Directors, submit your complete name (including maiden name for women), class year and a brief statement of several sentences covering your background and how you can contribute, to the address or email below NLT APRIL 20, 2013:

What type of help is needed by a new administration? 

Data Base and Directory Management- Knowledge and expertise to interact the ACSAA database with the ACSAA Website in a secure manner. The ACSAA Microsoft Access database has over 4000 alumni records of which between 400-500 alumni have been active members at any one time.  

Web Site Management - The ACSAA Website was down recently after our host got out of the business and we had to evaluate other providers before finding a new host.  Transitioning the Data Base and the publications to the website would require adding specialized software and implementing security enhancements to prevent hacking and protect sensitive information in the members only area. 

Gaucho Gazette Publication- If a newsletter format with class inputs is to be continued, one or more volunteers are needed to collect class rep and other administrative and reunion news inputs and compile them into a newsletter or blog format adaptable to the website.  As an alternative, ongoing blogs for individual class years or class year groupings could be set up, if not already existing, to facilitate alumni communications. A decision of how to handle members without Internet access would have to be made.   

Financial Management - Volunteer needed to take over Treasurer’s duties and overseeing dues collections, payment of expenses, and financial reports. 

Reunion Planning - It’s time to start planning the next ACSAA reunion, which would fall in the summer of 2015.   Considerable interest exists in having a reunion in the South, in particular the State of Texas.   Large alumni groups are located in various cities there.    Volunteers are needed to join an Organizing Committee once a site selection has been made.   

There is a lot on the plate to keep the ACSAA going.   We hope you will step forward to help Jade (York) Polk ’82 take the organization into the future and preserve the wonderful memories of our time in Argentina.  Your support will determine whether the ACSAA keeps its current organization structure or reverts back to an informal social organization.  

Sincerely,
